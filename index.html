Foreign transaction fees and currency conversion on credit cards

FX.Creditcard explains how foreign transaction fees, currency conversion rates and cross-border processing are documented when you use a credit card abroad or with international merchants.

Compare credit cards

What is a foreign transaction fee?

A foreign transaction fee is an additional charge that may apply when a purchase is processed in a currency different from your account currency, or routed through a non-domestic acquirer. Card documentation usually lists this as a percentage of the transaction amount, separate from interest and other fees.

Some cards advertise “no foreign transaction fees”, while others apply a surcharge on top of the underlying exchange rate. Understanding how this fee is defined in the pricing section helps you compare cards for international use.

How currency conversion and FX processing work

When you pay abroad, one of two main paths is typically used. Either the card network converts the transaction at its own wholesale rate, or the merchant offers dynamic currency conversion (DCC) and converts the amount into your home currency at a rate they set.

Issuer terms may explain whether network rates, issuer markups or merchant-provided rates are used, and when additional cross-border or FX fees apply. These details determine the real cost of using a card for travel and online purchases in other currencies.
